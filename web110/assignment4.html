I learned that spam emails can contain images that the sender can track, and when you open up the email, the image will load and will tell the spammer that the email works, which they can use to send more spam or add your email to other spammers.

I found out that you can turn off email images to load automatically, so I followed the guide on how to do that. Not only will I hopefully receive less spam in the future now, but now I will be able to possibly help someone else who is receiving an increased amount of spam, or who might be more at risk of falling for an email phising scam like my mother in law who didn't grow up using emails and as much knowledge about them.
