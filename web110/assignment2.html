I also chose to do the activity: Configure browser extensions.

I checked for the most popular add-ons to chrome. I already have installed a password manager called Bitwarden, and an ad blocker called uBlock Origin. I have been using them for a while and will stick with them. I did find an extension called CrxMouse Gestures and installed that into chrome. It lets you hold down the right click button and drag your mouse in a certain way to make a gesture that tells your browser to do something certain. For instance, now I can hold down the right click button and swipe left, it tells the browser to go back a page, and in reverse, if I hold down the button and swipe right, it tells the browser to go forward.

The password manager has been very useful and I'm sure will help in the future, as it has extensions for both chrome and firefox browsers and probably more, as well as my phone. It will be easy to keep track of passwords between multiple browsers or if I am working somewhere like the school's computer lab that does not have my passwords saved.
