The other activity I chose to do was to review the RIAA:About Piracy site and research legal issues and potential penalties related to illegal peer-to-peer file sharing.

I learned from the wikipedia page that: "In the case of file sharing networks, companies claim that peer-to-peer file sharing enables the violation of their copyrights. File sharing allows any file to be reproduced and redistributed indefinitely. Therefore, the reasoning is that if a copyrighted work is on a file sharing network, whoever uploaded or downloaded the file is liable for violating the copyright because they are reproducing the work without the authorization of the copyright holder or the law." This means that the user is responsible for making sure that the file they are downloading from a p2p site is not copyrighted, or else you can be charged for downloading it. I think that it is important that the media artists get paid for their work and if their work was out there being transfered all over for free they would not be able to keep creating their art. I used to download music and movies from p2p sites and not think much of it, but now I use streaming services like Spotify and Netflix instead.
