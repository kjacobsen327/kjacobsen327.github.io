Activity 2:

For the second activity, I chose to do the 7th activity, which was to research, identify and describe the necessary components required to set-up a home network for yourself.

The first thing and (probably most important) you need to set up a home network is a subscription with your internet service provider to provide a connection to your house. Around here, the options are only DSL, satellite, or cable, hopefully one day fiber will be an option. Then you need a modem, which turns the analog signal from the cable to a digital one. You can then connect a router to the modem, although sometimes they are built into the modem as well. The router will allow your devices to connect to each other on your home network, and also connect to the internet outside of your network. You can either plug in the devices to the router, but having a wifi router and wireless devices are the easier way to do it now. After getting the devices connected, you can make a workgroup through Windows, which makes it easier to share files and printers or other devices on your local network.

I will eventually plan on setting up a home network, I feel like it will be useful with a web development job which might have you work from home some days. Also my fiance is getting into graphic arts, so it would be nice to set up an easier way to share things over our home network.
